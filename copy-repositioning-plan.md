# SAIAI Copy Repositioning Plan

## Objective

Reposition the website to feel more minimal and practical, with first-person voice and clear execution-focused offers for small businesses and startups.

## Confirmed Decisions

1. Voice is first-person (`I`, `me`, `my`) across pages.
2. Audience language uses `small businesses` (not `local businesses`).
3. Keep `AI` as an important capability and support it with a dedicated offering.
4. Dedicated AI service name: `AI Workflow & Automation Sprint`.
5. Keep five services for now.

## Positioning Statement (Source Of Truth)

I work with small businesses and startups to design, build, and launch practical technical systems, including websites, marketing stack implementation, BI foundations, AI workflows, and general engineering projects.

## Messaging Principles

1. Be specific and outcome-oriented.
2. Avoid fractional CTO / executive-title framing.
3. Use plain language over strategy-heavy phrasing.
4. Emphasize hands-on build + launch + handoff.
5. Keep sections short and scannable.

## Service Architecture (Five Offers)

1. Website Design & Build
2. Marketing Stack Launch Support
3. BI & Reporting Foundations
4. AI Workflow & Automation Sprint
5. Engineering Consulting & Build Support

## Page-By-Page Copy Plan

## Homepage (`/`)

### Hero

Headline:
`I work with small businesses and startups to launch technical projects that actually ship.`

Supporting:
`From websites and marketing stack setup to BI foundations, automation, and AI workflows, I work hands-on from scoping through launch and handoff.`

CTAs:
`Book Intro Call` and `Get in Touch`

### Audience Section

Small Businesses:
`You need reliable technical execution without agency overhead. I can help build your web presence, improve lead flow, and set up systems your team can maintain.`

Startups:
`You need focused help on a high-priority initiative. I can step in for a scoped engagement and help you move from idea to production quickly.`

### Services Preview

Show all five services with concise summaries and practical outcomes.

### Process

1. Scope the real problem and define success clearly.
2. Build and launch the highest-leverage solution.
3. Hand off a working system with documentation and next-step guidance.

### Final CTA

`If you have a technical project that needs to move, send a short brief and I’ll recommend the best next step.`

## Services Page (`/services`)

### Goals

1. Make all five offers easy to scan in under 30 seconds.
2. Keep each card concrete: who it is for, what gets delivered, typical timeline.
3. Ensure AI offer has equal prominence with other services.

### Required Updates

1. Reframe any leadership-heavy/fractional framing into execution-first consulting language.
2. Standardize all service summaries to short, direct language.
3. Ensure service naming is consistent with the five-offer architecture above.

## AI Service Detail (`AI Workflow & Automation Sprint`)

### Core Framing

`I help teams apply AI where it improves a real workflow, not just where it sounds impressive.`

### Expectations

1. Start with one narrow workflow.
2. Integrate with real systems and data where possible.
3. Deliver a usable implementation and explicit next-step recommendation.

### Outcome Promise

`You leave with a working system, clear tradeoffs, and a realistic path forward.`

## About Page (`/about`)

### Goals

1. Keep founder credibility concise and relevant.
2. Emphasize breadth as a practical strength.
3. Clarify audience split without sounding exclusive.

### Draft Direction

`I’m a general-purpose technologist with startup experience across zero-to-one builds. Most of my engagements are with small businesses, and I also partner with startups when scope is clear and execution speed matters. I focus on practical delivery across websites, growth systems, BI, AI workflows, and engineering projects.`

## Copy QA Checklist

1. First-person voice used consistently.
2. `Small businesses` terminology used consistently.
3. No `fractional CTO` language remains.
4. AI appears both as a capability and dedicated service.
5. CTA language remains: `Book Intro Call` (primary), `Get in Touch` (secondary).
6. Every major section communicates scope, outcome, and fit.

## Implementation Sequence

1. Update global site messaging/config copy.
2. Update homepage section copy.
3. Update services index copy.
4. Update service detail content (especially leadership-oriented service copy).
5. Update about page copy.
6. Run final pass for consistency and brevity.
